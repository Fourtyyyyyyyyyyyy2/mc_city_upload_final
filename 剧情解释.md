# Story & Lore · The Soul Tree City

> This document tells the **story and worldview** behind the city (GDMC *Narrativity*).
> For the technical architecture and how to run the generator, see
> `GENERATOR_OVERVIEW.md` and `README_RUN.txt`.

---

## I. The Age of Bloom

At the center of the world grows a **Soul Tree** — legend says its roots are woven
into the memory of the land, and its cherry-blossom crown is the beating heart of
this place.

Four guilds founded a city around the Soul Tree, each holding a quarter of the land
and together guarding the sacred tree:

- **The Scholars** — keepers of knowledge and lore, who study the mysteries of the Soul Tree.
- **The Engineers** — masters of making and machinery, who raised the city's towers and devices.
- **The Merchants** — masters of trade and markets, who gathered the wealth of the four directions here.
- **The Adventurers** — masters of exploration and expedition, who guarded the city's frontier.

The city was laid out around the Soul Tree and its central plaza. Four grand axial
avenues reached outward, commercial streets lined the roads, districts spread like a
chessboard, and walls with watchtowers encircled it all. This was once a sacred
meeting place of knowledge, craft, trade, and courage.

## II. The Gaze Descends — The Cataclysm

No prosperity is eternal. Darkness came at once from both sky and sea:

- **The Eye King** — colossal, pitch-black warding idols, like watching gods, that
  hover above the city and stare coldly down upon its fate.
- **The Ocean Monster (Bloop)** — an immense creature slumbering in the water, whose
  silhouette can only be glimpsed from the seaward side of the city.
- **The Dark Colossus** — on the widest lands, giant figures emerge from the mountains
  themselves, standing to face the Soul Tree.

With them came an **invading army**. Columns of pillagers broke through the gates,
ravagers smashed the walls, and siege camps ringed the city until it was fully
surrounded. The city's **iron golem defenders** made their last stand inside the gates
and around the final line at the Soul Tree — but they could not hold back the tide.

The Soul Tree was severed at the root and set ablaze, its fire rising into the sky.
The walls split open in breach after breach. Scorched earth and smoldering embers
covered the streets, fireballs fell from above, and an endless storm raged through the
long night. This is a **city burned in invasion**.

## III. The Revenant's View — The Present

By the time the player arrives, everything has become ruin. You are a **latecomer, a
revenant** upon this land. Through the broken walls, the burning sacred tree, the
frozen clash of armies and defenders, and the Eye Kings suspended overhead, you can
read at a glance both the city's former glory and its final battle.

The story is told **primarily by the environment itself**: without reading a single
word, the layout of the ruins, the fire and ash, and the formations of attackers and
defenders *are* the story. The city also contains optional layers of books, signs, and
street names that fill in a finer timeline for players who choose to explore deeper.

### Reading the Story On-Site

| What you see | What it means |
|---|---|
| The great central tree, burnt and broken | The Soul Tree — the heart of the city, cut down in the invasion |
| Four surrounding districts of distinct buildings | The four founding guilds: Scholars, Engineers, Merchants, Adventurers |
| Black idols floating overhead | The Eye Kings — the watching powers behind the fall |
| A monstrous shape half-sunk offshore | The Ocean Monster that came from the sea |
| Giant figures rising from distant mountains | The Dark Colossi, facing the fallen tree |
| Breaches in the walls, siege camps ringing the city | Where and how the invaders broke in |
| Iron golems arrayed at the gates and the core | The city's defenders, in their last stand |
| Scorched ground, embers, fireballs, storm | The aftermath of the burning |
